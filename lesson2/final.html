Design Principles

Moroni Motta

Visual Hierarchy

National Geographic
www.nationalgeographic.com

The National Geographic website adheres to design principles by maintaining a balance in color contrast, creating a visually pleasing experience for users. The carefully chosen color palette delights the eyes, while the font styles are not only easy to read but also well-spaced between letters. Additionally, important elements such as titles, links, and paragraphs are thoughtfully positioned in the top-left, enhancing both visual appeal and user accessibility.

Rule of Thirds

Rolling Stone
www.rollingstone.com

Rolling Stone adeptly adheres to the rule of thirds, skillfully organizing elements within a grid of three columns, particularly noticeable in the navigation bar. Furthermore, the strategic placement of articles on the left side of the screen aligns with the natural tendency of users to focus on this area first, contributing to an intuitive and engaging user experience.

White Space and Clean Design

Apple
www.apple.com

Through strategic spacing between its sections, the Apple website adeptly applies design principles, ensuring a clean and uncluttered visual experience for users. The deliberate use of space allows for a seamless and organized presentation, enhancing user engagement and maintaining a visually pleasing interface.
